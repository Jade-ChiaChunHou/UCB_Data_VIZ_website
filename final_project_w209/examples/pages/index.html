Project Introduction

  • emoji_objects
    OBJECTIVE

    Our visualizations aim to call attention to the disparate impact COVID-19 has had in the United States based on race, socioeconomic factors, time, geography, and more. These charts are intended to inform viewers of what populations have been most affected by COVID-19 as the pandemic has progressed. The following dashboards examine what factors are associated with greater contagion and lethality of COVID-19. We hope this will provide useful context for those wishing to understand the impact of this pandemic in the United States more granularly.

    This virus has been devastating for everyone in this country. This visualization shows which populations have been affected the most and what factors have been correlated with negative COVID-19 outcomes. It is important to remember that this data shows what has happened and what patterns have emerged. It does not predict what will happen, nor does it ascribe causality to these associated statistics.

  • account_circle
    USERS

    We created this visualization for the benefit of two distinct groups of users:

    • First, we hope this visualization can be used by policymakers in Federal, State, and Local governments across the United States to enhance their understanding of the impacts of COVID-19 and inform equitable relief planning.
    • Second, we made this visualization for US citizens, in order to illuminate which Americans have shouldered the heaviest loads in this pandemic and show what regions, populations, and socioeconomic classes have been most affected.

Covid Cases and Deaths in US county

COVID-19 Dashboard - Cases and Deaths in US county

  • DESCRIPTION
The impact of COVID-19 has been felt across the United States at different times. This visualization shows the county level data on how the United States has been affected, looking into cases and deaths per 100,000 people. By adjusting the time aspect of this visualization one can see where COVID-19 landed first and which counties and regions it has spread to since. Click on the bubble chart to focus on a specific state, or select counties on the map by dragging a box around them.






Race and COVID-19 relationship

Racial Dashboard - Race and COVID-19 relationship

  • DESCRIPTION
The next section focuses on the disparate impact COVID-19 has had on white and nonwhite populations in the United States. The data shows that throughout the course of the pandemic nonwhite Americans have been more likely to contract and die from COVID-19 than white Americans. This is true at the state level, where the vast majority of states report higher proportions of cases and deaths by nonwhites than would be predicted by their population sizes, and it is true at the county level where counties with higher nonwhite populations are likely to have greater numbers of cases and deaths than otherwise similar counties with lower nonwhite populations. This trend was particularly strong in the early days of the pandemic but has reversed in the last several months. In fact, in November counties with higher white populations were hit hardest by COVID-19, both with regards to contagion and lethality. All of these factors are part of the complex story of how COVID-19 has progressed and what populations have been impacted.

The analysis presented in this section should not be interpreted as a declaration of causality. There are many explanations for why nonwhite populations have borne the disproportional brunt of this virus, including geographic reasons, population density, and socioeconomic factors. This analysis is merely acknowledging the existence of this disparity and informing viewers of how COVID-19 has progressed in the United States.








Socioeconomic status and COVID-19 relationship

Socioeconomic Status - Cases and Relationships with Economic Factors

  • DESCRIPTION
Here we investigate the influence of socioeconomic factors on Covid-19 cases and deaths. In the first visualization, we explore the nationwide distribution of three socioeconomic factors: poverty rates, unemployment rates, and household income, each of which can be chosen by using the drop-down button above the US map. Hovering the mouse over a county shows detailed information about the county, including the cumulative number of confirmed cases and deaths by the date selected by the month bar below the map. Users can also filter for a state to zoom in to the state and see the regional distribution. The socioeconomic data was sourced from the Economic Research Service.

In the second visualization, we have created plots for poverty rates, household income, and unemployment rates, each of which can be chosen by using the drop-down button above the graphs. Each point represents a county in the US, and hovering a mouse over a point in the plot shows more information about the county. We have included regression lines best fitted for each plot's samples to help visualize the underlying correlation between Covid-19 impact and selected socioeconomic factors. Users can filter outliers by using bars to adjust the range of the number of cases and deaths visible in the plots, and the regression lines are calculated based on the filtered samples. Detailed information of the regression lines can be found in the tooltip box available when hovering a mouse over the lines. The Covid-19 numbers presented in the visualization were sampled at the end of the month from March to November 2020.

The correlation plots show statistically significant correlations between socioeconomic factors and Covid-19 impact. In particular, the number of death is positively correlated with poverty and unemployment rates, suggesting that financially struggling people are more likely to suffer Covid-19 deaths.
